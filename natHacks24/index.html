How to read the brain electroencephalograph (EEG)

An EEG is a graph of brain waves displaying the current activity of the brain. Each brain wave represents a different type of brain activity. Brain waves are separated into five categories. Gamma Waves (>30Hz) - Gamma waves represent learning problem solving based tasks, cognitive processing, and alertness.
Beta Waves (13-30Hz) - Beta waves represent alert consciousness, excitement, and general thinking.
Alpha Waves (8-12Hz) - Alpha waves represent both physically and mentally relaxed.
Theta Waves (4-8Hz) - Theta waves represent creativity, deep states of consciousness, dreaming, and reduced consciousness.
Delta Waves (<4Hz) - Delta waves represent deep sleep, loss of awareness, and the natural state of bodily repair.

Due to the way our system works, individuals who possess neurodivergent thought processing may create inaccurate results. This is not a 100% accurate way to come to a conclusion about your own thought processing capabilities.
